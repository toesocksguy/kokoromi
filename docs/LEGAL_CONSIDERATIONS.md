# Legal Considerations

*Not legal advice. Consult an IP attorney for absolute certainty.*

---

## Core Principle

**Ideas and concepts are not copyrightable — only their specific expression is.**

Le Cunff's framework (Plus-Minus-Next, hypothesis-based experiments, Pact/Act/React/Impact, Steering Sheet decisions) is a methodology. Methodologies are not owned. We can implement it freely.

---

## What We Can Do

- Implement the framework in full
- Use the terminology ("tiny experiments", "pact", "plus-minus-next") — none are trademarked as of early 2026 (**verify at [USPTO](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks) before any major release**)
- Explain framework concepts in our own words
- Credit Le Cunff and link to her book and Ness Labs

## What We Cannot Do

- Copy exact text from the book
- Use her specific case studies or examples verbatim
- Use her original illustrations or diagrams
- Claim the framework as our own without attribution

## GPL-3.0 and Attribution

GPL-3.0 requires:
- Preserve the license notice in all copies
- Make source available when distributing modified versions
- State significant changes

It does not conflict with crediting Le Cunff's framework. We should continue to include the attribution note in the LICENSE file:

> This project implements principles from "Tiny Experiments: How to Live Freely in a Goal-Obsessed World" by Anne-Laure Le Cunff (Penguin Random House, 2025). Not affiliated with or endorsed by the author or publisher.

---

## Privacy (GDPR / CCPA)

The app collects zero user data. No accounts, no cloud storage, no telemetry, no analytics.

- **GDPR**: No personal data processed → minimal obligations. No privacy policy required for a zero-data app, but a brief statement ("all data stays on your device, we collect nothing") is good practice in the Play Store listing.
- **CCPA**: No personal data sold or shared → no disclosure required.

If cloud sync is ever added, a full privacy policy would be required before shipping.
